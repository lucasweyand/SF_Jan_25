    1.Design

    Sketch

    Our creative process begins with the rough sketching of basic forms, using simple geometry and curves as the foundation. We take inspiration from both the natural world and human-made objects. As we explore these shapes and influences, a concept gradually takes shape. Once an idea emerges, the original sketch of the piece is placed on paper.

    CAD

    In the next phase, the sketch is transferred into Computer-Aided Design (CAD) software. Here, the precise geometry, dimensions, proportions, and constraints of the design are established. During this stage, appropriate joinery methods are selected, and adjustments are made to ensure the design's feasibility. This step involves numerous iterations and refinements, focused on achieving visual balance and optimizing ergonomics.

    Material

    With the design requirements now established, the focus shifts to material selection. We exclusively use solid hardwood, carefully choosing wood species not only for their aesthetic qualities but also to ensure that their technical properties align with the functional demands of the design. Consideration is also given to the cut of the lumber and grain orientation, selecting rift-sawn wood for spindles and legs to provide added strength and uniformity, or opting for flat-sawn lumber to enhance figure along a face.

    2.Milling

    Layout

    Once the boards have been dedicated to a piece, layout begins. We prioritize grain direction which contributes to the overall strength of the piece and secondarily are considering aesthetics such as color and grain matching.

    Milling

    Once the layout is finalized, we proceed by breaking down the rough-cut lumber and milling it to the required thickness and width. At this stage, we also assess the natural imperfections and checks in the wood. To enhance stability and strength, butterfly keys are installed as needed.

    3.Build

    Profiles

    At this stage of the furniture-making process, the focus shifts to shaping the individual components. The process begins with a combination of traditional hand tools, such as planes and drawknives, alongside modern power saws to roughly shape each piece. This careful balance of old and new techniques ensures both accuracy and efficiency in the work. Cylindrical elements like legs and spindles, are turned by hand on the lathe. This hands-on approach brings the furniture closer to its final shape.

    Joinery

    Joinery is where the individual components of the piece come together, both physically and visually. Time-honored methods such as mortise and tenon, dovetail, and wedged scarf joints are used, each chosen based on its suitability for the specific application. This phase requires careful precision, as the strength and durability of the piece depend on the accuracy of these joints.

    Dry-Fit

    The dry fit is a critical checkpoint in the assembly process, where all the pieces are temporarily put together without glue. This allows us to verify the accuracy of our joinery, make any last-minute adjustments, and ensure the overall structure aligns perfectly with our design intent. The dry fit also provides an opportunity to assess the piece's balance, proportions, and ergonomics, giving us confidence in the final assembly. Any necessary refinements are made before moving on to the permanent joining phase.

    Sculpting

    In the sculpting phase, specialty cutting tools are introduced to create complex, flowing shapes that standard machining can't achieve. This hands-on process often involves carving and shaping by hand, bringing out the unique character of each piece. Every curve and angle is meticulously refined to achieve a seamless blend of form and function.

    Glue-Up

    Glue-up is when all components are permanently joined. We use wood-specific adhesives for strong bonds. Clamping pressure and alignment are carefully managed to ensure tight joints. The process is done in stages with drying time to prevent shifting. The result is a solid structure, ready for finishing.

    4.Finishing

    Sanding

    Sanding is where the surface of the piece is perfected. Starting with coarse grit sandpaper and gradually moving to finer grits, we smooth out any remaining tool marks and refine the wood to a silky finish. This process enhances the tactile quality of the piece, preparing it for the application of finishes. The careful attention to sanding ensures that the natural beauty of the wood grain is fully revealed, creating a surface that is not only pleasing to the touch but also visually pleasing.

    Finish

    Finishing is the final step, where oil and wax are applied. Natural oils enhance the wood's color and grain, while wax adds a soft sheen and extra protection. Once the finish is complete, any weaving is done, finalizing the piece.
